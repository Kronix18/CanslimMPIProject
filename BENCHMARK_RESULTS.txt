Karp-Flatt e=1.67 at p=2 reflects that MPI startup and orchestrator-idle overhead
exceeded the benefit of 1 active worker. This is expected in master-worker designs
where the orchestrator (Rank 0) does no computation. At 4+ processes the serial
fraction stabilizes to ~0.15–0.36 reflecting SQLite reads and MPI message overhead.

Metrics Explanation:
- Speedup:    T_1 / T_p — how many times faster with p processes vs baseline
- Efficiency: Speedup / p — fraction of ideal parallelization achieved (1.0 = perfect)
- Karp-Flatt: e = (1/S - 1/p) / (1 - 1/p) — estimated serial code fraction
  - e = 0.00 : perfectly parallel
  - e = 0.10 : 10% serial, 90% parallel
  - e > 1.00 : MPI overhead exceeds benefit (p=2 case above)
